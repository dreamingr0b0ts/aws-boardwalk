# Business Licensing

## Who needs a business license

Every business operating within Alpenglow city limits needs an annual business license,
including home-based businesses, mobile vendors, and short-term rental operators. Nonprofit
organizations are licensed at no charge but must still register.

## License fees

The standard annual business license fee is $75. Home-based businesses pay a reduced fee of
$40. Mobile food vendors pay $150, which includes one health-and-fire plan review per year.
Licenses run on the calendar year and renewals are due January 31; a $25 late fee applies
from February 1.

## Application process

Apply online or at the City Clerk's office. New applications require a Colorado Secretary of
State registration number, a state sales tax license, and — for food businesses — a county
health inspection certificate. Most licenses are issued within 7 business days. Zoning staff
review every application to confirm the business activity is allowed at the proposed address.

## Home occupation rules

Home-based businesses may not generate more than 10 customer visits per day, may not employ
more than one person who does not live in the home, and may not use more than 25 percent of
the dwelling's floor area. Exterior signage is limited to one non-illuminated sign of 2 square
feet.

## Changes and transfers

Business licenses are not transferable between owners. A change of location requires a new
zoning review but no additional fee within the same license year. Closing businesses should
notify the Clerk in writing to stop renewal notices.
