# Appeals Process

## What can be appealed

Decisions of the Building Official (code interpretations, permit denials, stop-work orders,
contractor registration suspensions) may be appealed to the Building Board of Appeals. Zoning
decisions (variances, use interpretations) go to the Board of Adjustment instead.

## Filing an appeal

File a written appeal with the City Clerk within 30 calendar days of the decision, stating
the decision appealed, the relief requested, and the code sections at issue. The filing fee
is $150, refunded in full if the appeal is upheld.

## Hearing procedure

The Building Board of Appeals hears cases within 45 days of filing. Hearings are public; the
appellant presents first, staff responds, and the board may question both. The board may
uphold, overturn, or modify the decision by majority vote of a five-member quorum. Written
findings are mailed within 10 business days.

## During an appeal

Filing an appeal stays enforcement of the decision except stop-work orders issued for
immediate life-safety hazards, which remain in effect. Permit clocks pause during the appeal.

## Further review

Board decisions are final administrative actions. Further review is by the district court
under Colorado Rule of Civil Procedure 106(a)(4), filed within 28 days of the board's written
decision.
